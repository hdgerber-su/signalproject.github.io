ROTC/OCS

You must have a college degree to be an officer. You can either do Reserve Officer Training Corps (ROTC) or Officer Candidate School (OCS).

ROTC is when you train to be an officer while in college, OCS is when you aquire your degree yourself and then join the military.

Being a Leader

When you are an officer you aren't the one kicking down doors necessarily. You role is to be a leader but in a more managerial type of way. Signal officers have to know their people and their equipment to be successful.

Signal Battalion

If you are a signal officer assigned to a signal battalion, you will be working with other signal soldiers for the majority of your work. Larger bases usually have signal battalion and this is a nice option as an officer because you are with your branch of people.

Signal Attachment

When you are a signal officer in charge of an attachment this means you will be working with combat arms. Everything the army does is in support to the infantry, and combat arm branches. This type of officer job will require more physically strenuous work and being out in the field often.
